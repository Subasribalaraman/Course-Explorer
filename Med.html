Popular Courses


Medical (MBBS) Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery

The MBBS, or Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery, is a comprehensive undergraduate medical degree. It typically lasts 5.5 years in India, including a 12-month compulsory internship, and prepares students to become qualified doctors

BDS Bachelor of Dental Surgery

The Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) is a 5-year undergraduate program in dental sciences, combining 4 years of academic study and a year of internship. It's the recognized professional degree for dentists in India and leads to the title "Dr.". The curriculum focuses on dental anatomy, physiology, pathology, and clinical dentistry, preparing students for diagnosing and treating various oral health conditions.

BAMS – Ayurvedic Medicine

The Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS) is a 5.5-year undergraduate degree program in India that combines the principles of Ayurvedic medicine with modern medical knowledge. It aims to train professionals who can diagnose, treat, and prevent health issues using Ayurvedic techniques while also understanding and integrating modern diagnostic and treatment methods

BHMS – Homeopathy

The BHMS, or Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine and Surgery, is a five-and-a-half-year undergraduate degree program in homeopathy, a system of alternative medicine. It prepares students to become licensed homeopathic practitioners, emphasizing a holistic approach to healthcare that considers the physical, mental, and emotional well-being of patient

B.Sc. Nursing

A B.Sc. Nursing is a four-year undergraduate program that prepares students for careers as registered nurses, providing them with the necessary knowledge and skills to work in various healthcare settings. The course emphasizes both theoretical and practical aspects of nursing, covering topics like anatomy, physiology, pharmacology, and patient care.

Radiology, Anesthesia Technology, Optometry, Emergency Care
  • Radiology

    • Radiology involves using medical imaging techniques such as X-rays, MRI, and CT scans to diagnose and monitor diseases. Radiology technologists assist doctors in capturing and interpreting images of the internal body structures.

    Career roles: Radiology Technician, MRI Technologist, CT Scan Specialist

  • Anesthesia Technology

    • Anesthesia technologists support anesthesiologists during surgical procedures. They help prepare and maintain anesthesia equipment, monitor patients under anesthesia, and ensure safety during operations.

    Career roles: Anesthesia Technician, Operation Theatre Assistant, ICU Technician

  • Optometry

    • Optometry deals with eye care. Optometrists diagnose vision problems, prescribe glasses/contact lenses, and detect eye-related diseases. They are primary eye care professionals (but not surgeons like ophthalmologists).

    Career roles: Optometrist, Vision Therapist, Optical Shop Consultant

  • Emergency Care (Emergency Medical Technology)

    • This field trains professionals to respond to medical emergencies like accidents, heart attacks, or trauma. They stabilize patients and provide first-line care during transport to hospitals.

    Career roles: Emergency Medical Technician (EMT), Ambulance Paramedic, Trauma Care Assistant
